Noise Generation Mechanisms of Slat Tracks

My MASc research investigates the aerodynamic mechanisms associated with noise emissions from aircraft high-lift slat systems.

Project overview

On an aircraft, the function of the leading-edge slat is to delay the onset of aerodynamic stall, increasing the maximum sectional lift coefficient of an airfoil. In order to understand how exactly this is achieved, I would recommend reading "High-Lift Aerodynamics" by A.M.O. Smith (1975). The leading-edge slat is deployed using slat tracks, which are built into the wing in order to transmit the lift and drag from the slat to the main element and resist pitching moments incurred at the slat.

This project combines wind-tunnel experimentation, acoustic measurements, aerodynamic analysis, and flow-field diagnostics to identify the dominant noise-generation mechanisms and evaluate potential mitigation concepts.

My contributions

  • Designed and evaluated parametric slat-track configurations
  • Conducted hybrid anechoic wind-tunnel testing
  • Processed microphone-array and aerodynamic measurements
  • Performed acoustic beamforming and spectral analysis
  • Analysed particle image velocimetry flow fields
  • Correlated aerodynamic structures with acoustic sources

Methods and tools

Wind-tunnel testing, microphone-array beamforming, particle image velocimetry, Python, MATLAB, signal processing, uncertainty analysis, and aerodynamic data reduction.

Industry collaboration

This research is being conducted at the University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies in collaboration with Bombardier .
